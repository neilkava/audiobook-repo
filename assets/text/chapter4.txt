CHAPTER 4: THE ARK BENEATH THE CLIFFS

By the time they reached the other side of the inlet, the rain had softened into mist.

The forest eventually opened into a narrow cove hidden between towering cliffs.

And there-

resting silently against the black water-

stood the ark.

It was smaller than Pom expected.

Not some enormous warship.

Just an old cedar vessel weathered by time and sea salt.

And hanging near the centre of the ark…

a single golden lamp swayed gently in the wind.

Still lit.

Its warm glow reflected softly across the dark water below.

For a moment, neither of them spoke.

Because somehow…

in this forgotten hidden corner of the world…

the tiny flame was still alive.

The ocean rocked the ark gently back and forth.

Nom stared at it like a child discovering treasure.

“…Pom,” he whispered.

“I KNOW,” she whispered back immediately, already reading his mind.

Something they called mind connect.

A narrow wooden ramp led onto the ark.

The wood creaked softly beneath their feet as they climbed aboard.

Inside, the ship smelled of weathered wood, rainwater, and old paper.

The same warm golden light from the hanging lamp drifted softly through the cabin.

Maps covered the tables.

Star charts.

Journals.

Strange symbols carved into wood darkened with age.

Pom brushed dust carefully from one of the journals.

“What does it say?” Nom asked softly.

Pom leaned closer to read.

“‘The lights do not guide you toward treasure,’” she read aloud.

Nom looked at her.

“Then what do they guide you toward?”

Pom slowly turned the page.

“…‘Toward what already feels like home.’”

Silence settled gently between them.

Outside, waves rolled softly against the ark.

Then suddenly-

a low sound echoed somewhere beyond the fog surrounding the ship.

Both of them froze.

The sound came again.

Closer this time.

Pom immediately moved nearer to Nom.

“…Okay,” she whispered nervously as she stared into the darkness. “That definitely sounded alive.”

Nom narrowed his eyes toward the fog as though preparing for battle despite being emotionally unprepared for most things.

The hanging lamp above them swayed softly in the wind.

Pom looked sideways at him for a second.

“…You’re still having fun right now, aren’t you?” she whispered.

Nom glanced back at her.

Warm golden light flickered softly across Pom’s face while ocean wind moved gently through her hair.

And despite the terrifying creature noises echoing somewhere beyond the fog…

Nom smiled a little.

“By the way,” he said quietly, “you’re really beautiful.”

Pom stared at him in disbelief.

“…Nom.”

“What?” he asked innocently.

“There is possibly a sea demon nearby.”

Nom leaned casually against the railing.

“There’s never a wrong time to tell you you’re beautiful.”

For a second, Pom just looked at him.

Then she chuckled softly, shaking her head while warmth spread across her cheeks beneath the golden light.

And somewhere out in the darkness…

the mysterious sea creature immediately lost all emotional significance.

A few moments later, Pom suddenly leaned down and bit Nom’s arm.

Nom looked at the bite mark.

Then at Pom.

Pom looked back innocently.

Nom nodded once.

“…Fair enough.”

“You barely react anymore,” Pom observed proudly.

Nom rubbed his arm thoughtfully.

“I’m used to it now,” he replied calmly. “I kinda like it.”

Pom smiled so hard she had to look away.
