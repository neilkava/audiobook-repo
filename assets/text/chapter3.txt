CHAPTER 3: BLACK WATER CROSSING

For nearly twenty minutes, the forest trail twisted deeper through rain and fog while Nom followed the faded markings on the map with growing excitement.

Then suddenly-

Nom stopped walking so fast that Pom bumped directly into his back.

“Nomaaaa…”

“…Pom.”

“What?”

Nom slowly pointed ahead through the trees.

Hidden between the cliffs stood a long wooden bridge stretching across black water far below.

Pom immediately grabbed the map from his hands.

There, near the edge of the parchment, was a tiny hand-drawn sketch of the exact same bridge.

For a moment, both of them just stared at it.

“…Okay that’s actually crazy,” Pom whispered.

Nom looked one emotional step away from combusting from excitement.

“POM THE MAP IS REAL.”

“Keep your voice down!”

Rain drifted softly through the cedar trees around them as they slowly stepped closer to the bridge.

Mist floated above the water beneath them in pale silver ribbons.

And somewhere far beyond the fog…

a faint warm light glowed briefly in the distance.

Pom stopped walking.

“…Nom,” she whispered softly.

Nom stared out into the darkness.

“I see it.”

The light flickered once through the mist before disappearing again.

The bridge creaked softly beneath their feet as cold wind swept across the water.

Nom instinctively stepped slightly closer to Pom.

Not enough for most people to notice.

But Pom noticed everything.

“You always stand closer to me when something scary appears,” she observed quietly.

Nom continued staring ahead at the bridge as though conducting highly advanced tactical analysis.

“That is strategic positioning.”

“Strategic?” Pom asked suspiciously.

“Yes,” Nom replied confidently. “As taught to me directly by Master Roshi.”

Pom narrowed her eyes slightly.

“…You’re scared.”

Nom politely corrected her.

“I’m alert.”

The bridge suddenly groaned loudly beneath them.

Nom grabbed the rope railing instantly before carefully testing the wooden plank ahead with one cautious foot.

Pom watched him quietly for a second.

“…This is my favourite flavour of Nomi.”

Nom blinked.

“What does that mean?”

“Acoustic Nom,” Pom revealed proudly.

Nom stared at her in complete confusion.

“…I don’t know what that means.”

“You’re not supposed to,” Pom chuckled softly.

Another loud creak echoed through the bridge.

Nom slowly looked down at the violently shaking wooden planks beneath them.

“…Okay see THAT sound was unnecessary.”

Pom burst into laughter so suddenly she nearly lost balance herself.

Nom immediately grabbed her arm to steady her before she slipped.

For a second, both of them froze slightly at how close they suddenly were.

Cold wind drifted quietly through the bridge ropes around them.

Far away, the faint golden light flickered once again through the fog.

And somewhere beyond the cliffs ahead…

something waited for them in the darkness.
