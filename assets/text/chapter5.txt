CHAPTER 5: WHERE THE OCEAN GLOWED

Outside, the wind began changing.

The ocean had grown strangely still.

Nom stepped slowly onto the deck.

Pom followed beside him.

The fog above the sea had begun drifting apart.

And then-

the sky came alive.

Green light spread slowly across the darkness above them like enormous rivers flowing through the stars.

The aurora shimmered silently over the ocean.

Waves reflected glowing streaks of emerald and gold across the water below.

And suddenly the sea itself looked filled with drifting lanterns.

The Lantern Sea.

For a moment, neither of them moved.

Then Nom suddenly turned toward Pom with the pure excitement.

“POM WE FOUND IT.”

Before Pom could react, Nom grabbed her carefully and spun her once across the deck as lantern light and aurora shimmered around them.

Pom burst into startled laughter.

“Nom!”

“We actually found an ancient magical glowing ocean,” Nom said, still sounding completely amazed. “THIS IS THE BEST DAY OF MY LIFE.”

Pom laughed so hard she had to grab Nom’s shoulders to steady herself.

The aurora shimmered softly above them while lantern light danced across the waves below.

And somewhere beneath all the excitement, somewhere quieter that Nom rarely spoke from out loud…

he felt something warm settle gently in his chest.

He had crossed forests, suspicious puddles, collapsing bridges, and possibly haunted oceans searching for the Lantern Sea…

but somehow every beautiful thing in the world felt brighter whenever Pom was beside him.

Nom would never fully figure out how she did that.

And honestly…

he wasn’t sure he wanted to.

Neither of them spoke for a while after that.

There are some kinds of beauty that silence understands better than words.

The ark drifted slowly across the glowing water.

Wind whispered softly through the sails overhead.

“…Worth the puddle?” Pom asked as she stepped beside him near the edge of the deck.

Nom considered this seriously.

“…The puddle remains suspicious.”

Pom laughed quietly.

Then suddenly-

thick fog rolled violently across the water.

The wind shifted hard.

The clanging lamps hanging from the mast swung sharply overhead.

For one terrifying moment, Pom disappeared completely into the mist.

Nom’s expression changed instantly.

“Pom?” he called out immediately.

No answer.

The fog swallowed the deck around him.

“Poma?” Nom called again, his voice quieter this time.

Then finally-

“Don’t worry… I’m here.”

A hand found his sleeve gently through the fog.

Nom exhaled quietly.

“…Okay.”

The tension left his shoulders immediately.

The fog slowly drifted away again.

The aurora returned overhead in soft waves of green light.

Pom stood quietly beside him as the aurora shimmered across the ocean.

Cold wind drifted softly through the sails overhead.

For a while, neither of them spoke.

Then Pom looked up at him gently.

“You know…” she said quietly, “I think this is the happiest I’ve ever seen you.”

Nom looked out across the Lantern Sea for a moment before smiling softly to himself.

“…Yeah,” he admitted. “I think it might be.”

The green light reflected softly across the water beneath them.

And somewhere deep down, beneath all the excitement and adventure and impossible glowing oceans…

Nom realized something quietly.

Every frightening place had somehow felt smaller the moment Pom walked into it beside him.

That was the real reason he kept stepping forward.

Even when he was scared.

Pom rested her head gently against his shoulder.

“And?” she asked softly.

Nom looked out across the Lantern Sea one last time.

“…I think I found what I was looking for a long time ago.”

And beneath the ancient northern lights…

the Lantern Sea carried them quietly into the night.
